Meet Eliud Kipchoge!

Eliud Kipchoge is a Kenyan long-distance runner who has established himself as one of the greatest athletes of all time. Born in Kapsisiywa, Kenya, on November 5, 1984, Kipchoge began his career as a track athlete, competing in various competitions such as the World Championships and the Olympics. He won a bronze medal in the 5,000 meters at the 2004 Athens Olympics and a silver medal in the same event at the 2008 Beijing Olympics. Born in Kapsisiywa, Kenya, on November 5, 1984, Kipchoge began his career as a track athlete, competing in various competitions such as the World Championships and the Olympics. He won a bronze medal in the 5,000 meters at the 2004 Athens Olympics and a silver medal in the same event at the 2008 Beijing Olympics. In 2013, Kipchoge transitioned to marathon running and quickly made his mark in the sport. He won his first major marathon in Hamburg, Germany, in 2013 and went on to win the Chicago Marathon in 2014. He has since won several major marathons, including the London Marathon, the Berlin Marathon, and the Amsterdam Marathon.


Kipchoge is perhaps best known for his record-breaking performance in the 2019 Vienna Marathon, where he became the first person to run a marathon in under two hours. Although the run was not an official world record, it was a historic achievement and demonstrated Kipchoge's incredible endurance and skill as a runner In addition to his athletic achievements, Kipchoge is also a philanthropist and is involved in several charitable organizations aimed at promoting education and sports in his native Kenya. He has also been an advocate for environmental conservation, using his platform as a world-renowned athlete to raise awareness about the importance of sustainability and protecting the planet. Overall, Eliud Kipchoge is a true icon of the sport of running and a role model for aspiring athletes around the world. His achievements, both on and off the track, have made him a true legend in the world of sports.
